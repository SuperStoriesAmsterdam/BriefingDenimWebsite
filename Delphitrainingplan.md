# Delphi Training Plan
### Goal: The World's Most Knowledgeable Denim Advisor

---

## North Star

By the end of this training plan, the Denim City Delphi concierge should be able to do what no other AI in the world can: speak about denim with the combined authority of the people who built the global denim industry — from the sustainable laundry labs of Amsterdam to the shuttle looms of Kojima, Japan.

A visitor asking "why does selvedge denim fade differently?" should get an answer that carries the weight of Momotaro craftsmanship philosophy, Andrew Olah's industry perspective, and Adriano Goldschmiedt's technical knowledge — filtered through Denim City's voice.

---

## How Delphi Learns: Core Principles

Before the plan, understand how to feed Delphi well.

**Delphi ingests:**
- PDF and text documents
- Audio files (interviews, podcasts, recordings)
- Video (uploaded or YouTube links)
- Website URLs (live scraping)
- Google Docs / markdown

**What trains it best:**

| Content type | What it gives the AI |
|---|---|
| Structured FAQs and service docs | Factual accuracy and precision |
| Long conversational interviews | Voice, personality, nuance |
| Deep expert monologues | Domain authority and depth |
| Stories and testimonials | Emotional resonance and relatability |
| Press articles and reviews | Third-party credibility |

**Golden rules:**
1. **Breadth before depth** — cover all topics at a basic level first, then go deep. A concierge that knows something about everything is more useful than one that knows everything about one thing.
2. **Conversational over structured** — long, flowing speech trains better than bullet-point Q&A. Let experts talk freely.
3. **Repetition builds confidence** — the same fact said in five different ways, by five different people, makes the AI more confident and nuanced in how it answers.
4. **Fill gaps before adding depth** — after each phase, test the AI and find what it can't answer. Fill those gaps before adding more depth.
5. **Stories are sticky** — a concrete repair story ("a 1987 Levi's with a blown-out knee, repaired with Sashiko stitching") trains the AI to answer with specificity, not generalities.

## Phase 1 — Foundation
**Weeks 1–2 | Priority: Critical**

This is the layer that makes the concierge useful from day one. All factual, all structured. Get this right before adding anything else.

### Documents to produce and upload

**1.1 Master FAQ document**
The single most important training document. Write it as if a smart, friendly team member is answering every possible visitor question. Not short answers — full, warm, conversational answers.

Topics to cover:
- What is Denim City? What is House of Denim? What is the relationship?
- Where are you? How do I get there? Parking? Public transport?
- Opening hours, pricing, what's free vs. what costs money
- What can I do in an hour? In a half day? In a full day?
- Is it suitable for kids? For seniors? For non-denim people?
- Every tour question: what's included, languages, group sizes, private tours
- Every course question: beginner vs. advanced, what you make, what you take home, booking process
- Every repair question: what can be repaired, pricing, walk-in vs. appointment, turnaround time
- The store: what brands, can I buy online (no), fabric by the meter
- The Lab: what it does, who it's for, how to get involved
- Venue rental: capacity, what's included, how to request a quote
- The Nudie and KOI free-repair partnerships: who qualifies, what's covered

**1.2 Services and pricing sheet**
Every service, every price tier, every booking link. Dry but essential. Keep updating this as prices change.

**1.3 Course catalog**
One document per course category. For each course:
- Name and one-line hook
- Duration
- What you make / what you learn
- Who it's for (experience level, age, group size)
- What's included (materials, tools, instruction language)
- Price and how to book
- What makes this course unique

**1.4 Amsterdam logistics document**
For international visitors:
- Address and exact entrance (De Hallen can be confusing)
- Nearest tram/metro/bus stops
- Cycle routes
- From Amsterdam Centraal: step-by-step
- Parking options
- Nearby lunch, coffee, and attractions for visitors combining with other things

**1.5 Brand glossary**
Denim terms the concierge must know cold:
- Selvedge / selvage
- Sanforized vs. unsanforized
- Raw / dry denim
- Indigo dyeing
- Warp and weft
- Weight (oz)
- Shuttle loom vs. projectile loom
- Deadstock fabric
- LWG (Leather Working Group equivalent for denim)
- Greenwashing vs. genuine sustainability
- Key certifications (GOTS, bluesign, etc.)

## Phase 2 — Voice & Personality
**Weeks 3–4 | Priority: High**

This is what makes the concierge sound like Denim City, not like a generic AI. The facts are there from Phase 1 — now give it the personality.

### Interviews to conduct

**2.1 Denim City floor staff**
The people who talk to visitors every day know what visitors actually ask. Interview them freely:
- What's the first thing people say when they walk in?
- What question do you get ten times a day?
- What surprises visitors most?
- What's the story you love telling most?
- When did you feel most proud of what happens here?

2–3 people, 30–45 minutes each. Audio is fine. Unscripted.

**2.2 Tour guides**
The tour script itself (upload this), plus a free interview:
- What moment in the tour do people respond to most?
- What question almost no one asks but everyone should?
- What do you wish visitors knew before they arrived?
- Tell me about a tour that you won't forget.

**2.3 Repair atelier staff**
The craft philosophy:
- What does "Zero Glue Tolerance" mean to you, in practice?
- What's the most interesting repair you've ever done?
- What can be saved that people think is gone?
- What's the philosophy behind Sashiko repair vs. invisible mending?
- Why does it matter that we repair instead of replace?

**2.4 Upload: all existing brand copy**
Once the website is live — upload the full site via URL. Before that, upload:
- All finalized PRD copy blocks
- Any existing brochures, tour descriptions, course descriptions
- Social media captions (these carry brand voice in concentrated form)
- Any existing email newsletters

## Phase 3 — Industry Authority
**Weeks 5–10 | Priority: High**

This is the layer that makes the concierge extraordinary. These interviews are the reason no other brand in the world could build this AI. Take time to prepare these conversations well.

### Interview: Andrew Olah
*Founder of Kingpins Show — the world's most influential denim trade show.*

Andrew carries a panoramic view of the global denim industry across decades. His knowledge gives the concierge the ability to speak with authority about where denim is going, not just what Denim City sells.

**Interview themes:**
- The history of denim as a global industry — how we got here
- What has genuinely changed in sustainability, and what is still greenwashing?
- What does the next decade look like for denim manufacturing?
- What role can innovation hubs like Denim City play in the industry's transformation?
- Who are the people and brands doing the most important work right now?
- What does the global denim supply chain look like to someone who has seen it all?
- What do consumers not understand about how their jeans are made?
- What excites you most about where denim is going?

**Format:** Long, conversational. Let him talk. 60–90 minutes. Audio or video. No bullet points in the response — just let him speak.

**What this gives the concierge:** The ability to contextualize Denim City within the global industry. When a brand asks "why should we work with Denim City's lab?" the concierge can answer from a position of industry perspective, not just product description.

---

### Interview: Adriano Goldschmiedt
*Pioneer in sustainable denim production and R&D (Canatiba and beyond).*

Adriano's knowledge is technical and transformative. He understands how denim is actually made, at industrial scale, and what sustainability really means at the fibre and chemistry level.

**Interview themes:**
- What is actually happening in the sustainable laundry process? What makes it genuinely sustainable vs. a claim?
- What did years of denim mill R&D teach you about what's possible?
- What innovations in washing and finishing do you believe in most?
- What is the biggest lie in denim sustainability marketing right now?
- What would you want a denim brand to understand before they approach a sustainable production partner?
- What does a truly circular denim product look like?
- How do you see Denim City's lab fitting into a brand's production journey?
- What are the questions brands should be asking that they're not?

**What this gives the concierge:** Technical depth for Lab & Atelier conversations. When a fashion brand lands on the site and asks about prototype development, the AI can speak at a level that earns respect — not just describe a service.

---

### Interview: Tony Tonnaer
*Founder of Kings of Indigo — one of the most respected sustainable denim brands.*

Tony has built something rare: a denim brand that is genuinely sustainable without being preachy. His brand-builder perspective is essential.

**Interview themes:**
- What made you decide to build a denim brand around sustainability as a core — not as a feature?
- What does "Towards a Brighter Blue" mean to you from a brand perspective?
- What separates real sustainability from marketing in denim?
- What has the consumer relationship with denim changed over your career?
- What do you look for in a manufacturing or education partner?
- What role does repair play in a truly sustainable denim future?
- What advice would you give a young designer entering the denim world today?
- What does Denim City mean to the denim ecosystem in Amsterdam?

**What this gives the concierge:** The brand-builder and sustainability voice. Essential for conversations with conscious consumers, emerging designers, and corporate sustainability teams looking to partner.

### Interview: Founder of Nudie Jeans
*Co-founder of Nudie Jeans — the Swedish brand that made sustainable denim mainstream.*

Nudie Jeans is one of the most important brands in the history of conscious denim. They pioneered free repairs as a brand promise before it was fashionable, built a global network of repair shops, and proved that transparency and sustainability could be the core of a commercially successful denim brand — not just a niche position. Crucially: Denim City is an official Nudie repair partner. This relationship gives the concierge real, lived context for what the partnership means in practice.

**Interview themes:**
- What made you build the free repair promise into the Nudie brand from the beginning?
- What did you learn from a decade of running free repair as a commercial model?
- What does it actually take to make denim that's worth repairing — at the design and construction level?
- What surprised you most about how customers responded to the repair program?
- What is the relationship between transparency and trust in a fashion brand?
- How do you think about the lifecycle of a Nudie garment — from first wear to final repair?
- What does the partnership with Denim City mean to you? What makes it work?
- What would you tell a young denim brand that wants to do this seriously?
- Where do you think the denim industry is in its sustainability journey — honestly?

**Why this is especially powerful for the concierge:**
Every visitor who arrives with a Nudie pair of jeans — and there will be many — should get an answer that reflects the philosophy behind that garment. The concierge can explain the free repair partnership not just as a policy but as a shared set of values between two organizations. That's a very different conversation from "yes, we repair Nudie for free."

## Phase 4 — Japanese Mastery
**Weeks 11–14 | Priority: Unique — do not skip**

This is the layer that makes the concierge genuinely the most knowledgeable denim advisor in the world. Japanese denim culture and craft represents the deepest tradition in the contemporary denim world — and almost no Western brand can speak to it from the inside.

### Interview: Founder of Momotaro Denim
*Kojima, Okayama — the heartland of Japanese selvedge denim.*

Momotaro Denim (Japan Blue Group) is one of the world's most respected premium denim brands. Their production philosophy is rooted in a tradition of craftsmanship that is centuries old, adapted to denim through generations of Kojima artisans.

**Why this matters for the concierge:**
- International visitors to Denim City — especially from Japan, the US, and Europe — are often serious denim people. They want to talk about selvedge, fade culture, raw denim, shuttle looms. The ability to speak authentically about Japanese denim tradition is a signal that the concierge is operating at a different level.
- The Fabric Wall in the store stocks Japanese selvedge. The concierge needs to be able to explain *why* that matters — not just that it's there.
- Japanese denim philosophy aligns perfectly with the repair and longevity ethos at the core of Denim City's mission.

**Interview themes:**
- What is the philosophy behind making denim in Kojima? What is the craft tradition?
- What makes Japanese selvedge denim different — technically and philosophically?
- What does "fade" mean to a Japanese denim enthusiast? How is that relationship with a garment different from how Western consumers think about clothes?
- What is a shuttle loom, and why does it matter?
- What role does indigo play in Japanese textile tradition beyond denim?
- What do you think Western denim culture understands about Japanese denim — and what does it miss?
- What is your philosophy on repair? On owning fewer, better things?
- What does it take to make denim that deserves to be repaired?
- What excites you about the future of Japanese denim craft?

**Supplementary Japanese denim documents to upload:**
- History of Kojima as Japan's denim capital
- The story of how American denim came to Japan post-WWII and was reimagined
- Selvedge denim production process — technical overview
- Japanese fade culture: the community, the philosophy, what "earned fades" means
- Overview of major Japanese denim mills (Kurabo, Kaihara, Nihon Menpu)
- The concept of *monozukuri* (the art/spirit of making things) and how it applies to denim

**What this gives the concierge:** The ability to speak to the most serious denim people in the world at their level. When a Japanese tourist walks into Denim City and starts talking about their raw denim journey, the concierge can meet them there. When a buyer from a premium retailer asks about the Fabric Wall's Japanese selvedge selection, the answer carries real depth.

## Phase 5 — Deep Denim Knowledge
**Weeks 15–18 | Priority: Medium-High**

The broad knowledge base that makes the concierge educated on any denim topic that might come up.

### Documents to produce and upload

**5.1 Denim history: a narrative overview**
- The origin of denim (Nîmes, de Nîmes → denim)
- Levi Strauss and the Gold Rush
- Denim's journey from workwear to youth culture to global fashion
- The Japanese reinvention of American denim
- The sustainability crisis: how the denim industry became one of the world's most polluting
- The current transformation: where innovation is happening

**5.2 How denim is made: full technical overview**
- Cotton farming: conventional vs. organic vs. recycled
- Spinning and weaving: shuttle looms vs. projectile looms
- Indigo dyeing: natural vs. synthetic, ring-dyeing, rope-dyeing
- Sanforizing vs. unsanforized
- Finishing: washes, treatments, distressing techniques
- What "sustainable" means at each stage

**5.3 The science of fading**
- Why indigo sits on the surface of the yarn (ring-dyeing)
- How wear patterns create honeycombs, whiskers, train tracks
- What raw/dry denim communities know about earned fades
- How washing and treatment affect fade patterns
- Why Momotaro and Japanese brands approach this differently

**5.4 Repair and longevity**
- The environmental case for repair (LCA data, water saved, CO2 avoided)
- Types of denim repair: invisible, decorative, structural
- Sashiko: the tradition, the technique, the philosophy
- How repair extends the life of a garment by decades
- The relationship between quality construction and repairability

**5.5 Sustainability deep-dive**
- Water usage in conventional denim production (7,000+ litres per pair)
- Chemical pollution: what the denim industry releases, and how this is changing
- Ozone washing, laser distressing, waterless finishing
- Deadstock fabric: what it is, why it matters, the limits of it as a solution
- Recycled denim: mechanical vs. chemical recycling
- Certification landscape: what GOTS, bluesign, and others actually mean

**5.6 The denim collector's world**
- What makes a pair of jeans collectible
- The community: forums, Instagram, flea markets, vintage dealers
- Key vintage references: 501xx, Type I, II, III jackets, the big E
- What denim people look for in a repair shop
- How to authenticate vintage Levi's

## Phase 6 — Social Proof
**Ongoing from Phase 1**

Start collecting this from day one. Feed it continuously.

- **TripAdvisor and Google reviews** — upload quarterly batches. Real visitor language is training gold.
- **Course graduate testimonials** — 2–3 paragraphs each: who they were, what they made, how it changed them.
- **Repair stories** — with customer permission: what was the garment, what was wrong, what was done, what the result felt like.
- **Press articles** — every piece of coverage. Upload as PDF or URL.
- **Student Instagram posts and captions** — captures the emotional response in the students' own words.
- **Incubator alumni stories** — what they built after Denim City. These are proof of the education's depth.

## Phase 7 — Continuous Refinement Loop
**Permanent — start after Phase 2**

### Monthly review process

1. **Pull the top 20 questions the concierge couldn't answer well** — Delphi shows you where it's uncertain or where users were unsatisfied.
2. **Identify the knowledge gap** — is it a missing document? A topic we never covered? A service that changed?
3. **Produce the filling content** — a new doc, a new interview clip, an updated price sheet.
4. **Upload and re-test.**
5. **Repeat.**

### Trigger-based updates

| Trigger | Action |
|---|---|
| New course added | Update course catalog doc |
| Price change | Update services and pricing sheet |
| New brand in store | Add to store brands document |
| New press coverage | Upload article |
| New Denim Dates event | No action needed if site is synced, otherwise manual update |
| New board member or interview | Add to interview queue |
| New tour testimonial batch | Upload |
| New sustainability research | Add to Phase 5 deep-knowledge docs |

## Interview Preparation Checklist

For each expert interview:

- [ ] Brief the interviewee on what Delphi is and how the interview will be used (to train an AI that speaks in their knowledge's voice — they should be comfortable with this)
- [ ] Share 5–7 themes in advance, not specific questions — let them think, not rehearse
- [ ] Record audio or video — 60–90 minutes minimum
- [ ] Let them speak freely — resist the urge to interrupt or redirect too quickly
- [ ] Ask follow-up prompts: "Can you say more about that?" / "What's a concrete example?" / "What would you say to someone who's never thought about this?"
- [ ] After recording: have it transcribed (Whisper, Otter.ai, or similar)
- [ ] Upload both the audio/video AND the transcript to Delphi — dual format reinforces training
- [ ] Tag the interview with the speaker's name and expertise area in Delphi's knowledge system
